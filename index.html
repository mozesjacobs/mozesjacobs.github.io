I am a Shanahan Foundation Predoctoral Researcher working at the AI Institute for Dynamics Systems at the University of Washington (UW). I graduated with a B.S. in Computer Science from UW in June 2022. I am currently applying to PhD programs in AI and Computer Vision. I am primarily interested in generative modeling. During my undergrad, I worked with Dr. Rajesh Rao in the Neural Systems Lab and with Dr. William Noble in the Noble Lab.

Current Work

Governing equations can be considered as a fundamental type of generative model. Currently, machine learning approaches can discover governing equations that describe deterministic dynamical systems. However, current approaches are not as well suited to model stochastic dynamics. I am developing approaches to discover the parameters of stochastic differential equations and generate new stochastic trajectories. I developed HyperSINDy, a method that uses hypernetworks and amortized variational inference to discover parameters of differential equations (i.e. the Lorenz system).
