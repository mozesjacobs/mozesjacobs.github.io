Hello! I am a first-year computer science PhD student at Harvard University, advised by Professor Demba Ba. My research interests are primarily in computer vision and generative modeling. I am studying how we can build build visual systems that fully understand visual scenes, which involves being able to decompose scenes into objects and their relationships. I'm particularly intrigued by problems like image generation (e.g. text-to-image diffusion models) and video generation. I am curious how we can bake inductive biases into generative models for these problems.

Past Projects

Gradient Origin Predictive Coding

I did my Undergraduate Honors Thesis, ``Gradient Origin Predictive Coding'', under Dr. Rajesh Rao. In short, I created an encoder-less VAE that performed inference using the gradient of the variational loss. You can find the thesis document here.

PASTIS

In the Noble Lab, I helped develop PASTIS, a Poisson-based algorithm that infers 3-D chromatin structures from Hi-C contact count maps.

Probabilistic Representations in the Brain

In the summer of 2020, I completed a research fellowship at the Center for Neurotechnology. I investigated evidence of probabilistic representations in the mouse brain using the primary neuropixels dataset from this paper by Dr. Nick Steinmetz. You can find my final presenation, delivered at the 2020 UW Summer Undergraduate Research Symposium, here.
